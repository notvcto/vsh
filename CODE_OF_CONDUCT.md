# VSH Community Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

VSH is a shell for everyone. To make that vision real, we commit to making participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

**We're building something inclusive. That starts with us.**

## Our Standards

### ‚úÖ Behaviors that contribute to a positive environment:

- **Be welcoming** - Use inclusive language, welcome newcomers warmly
- **Be patient** - Remember everyone is learning at their own pace
- **Be respectful** - Value diverse opinions and experiences
- **Be constructive** - Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully
- **Be empathetic** - Understand others' perspectives and situations
- **Be collaborative** - Work together toward the best outcomes
- **Be humble** - Acknowledge when you're wrong and learn from mistakes

### ‚ùå Unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, trolling, or discriminatory comments
- Personal attacks or insults
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Sexual language or imagery in any community spaces
- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Our Responsibilities

**Core team members** are responsible for:
- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that don't align with this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:
- In all VSH community spaces (GitHub, Discord, community calls, etc.)
- When representing VSH in public spaces
- Online and in person

Examples of representing VSH:
- Using an official VSH email address
- Posting via official social media accounts
- Acting as a representative at events
- Wearing VSH merchandise at public events

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

**Immediate Issues (Discord):**
- DM any online moderator
- Use the report function

**GitHub Issues:**
- Email: conduct@vsh.dev
- DM a core team member
- Use GitHub's report abuse feature

**All reports are confidential.** We will never share reporter identity without permission.

### What to Include in a Report

- Your contact info (so we can follow up)
- Names/usernames of people involved
- Description of what happened
- Links to relevant content
- Any additional context
- Whether you want to remain anonymous to the accused

### Response Process

1. **Acknowledgment** - Within 24 hours
2. **Investigation** - Core team reviews all available information
3. **Decision** - Core team decides on appropriate action
4. **Communication** - We inform the reporter of the outcome
5. **Appeal** - Decisions can be appealed to founder (Vic)

### Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity, consequences may include:

**1. Correction**
- **Action:** Private written warning
- **When:** Inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior
- **Effect:** Clarification of standards, request to improve

**2. Warning**
- **Action:** Formal warning with consequences for continued behavior
- **When:** Single violation or series of minor violations
- **Effect:** Temporary restrictions may be imposed

**3. Temporary Ban**
- **Action:** Temporary removal from all community spaces
- **When:** Serious violation or pattern of inappropriate behavior
- **Duration:** Typically 7-30 days
- **Effect:** No interaction with community during ban period

**4. Permanent Ban**
- **Action:** Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **When:** Severe violations (harassment, doxxing, threats) or repeated violations after temporary bans
- **Effect:** Permanent exclusion from all VSH spaces

### Transparency

- Enforcement decisions are documented privately
- Patterns and statistics shared with community (anonymized)
- Serious bans announced publicly to warn community
- Individual details kept confidential unless reporter agrees otherwise

## Special Considerations

### Technical Disagreements

**Healthy debate is encouraged!** You can:
- Strongly disagree with technical decisions
- Argue for different approaches
- Critique ideas and proposals

Just remember:
- Attack the idea, not the person
- Stay professional and respectful
- Accept that consensus isn't always possible
- Trust the RFC and governance process

**Example - Good:**
> "I disagree with using `nom` for parsing. Here's why I think `pest` is better: [technical reasons]. What do others think?"

**Example - Not Good:**
> "Only an idiot would choose `nom`. Anyone who supports this doesn't understand parsers."

### Beginners

VSH explicitly welcomes beginners. That means:

- **No "RTFM" responses** - If documentation exists, link it kindly
- **No "just Google it"** - Help them learn how to find information
- **No gatekeeping** - Everyone was new once
- **Celebrate learning** - Every question is an opportunity to improve docs

**Example - Good:**
> "Great question! This is explained in the [parser docs](link), but I can see why it's confusing. Here's a simpler explanation: [explanation]. Let me know if that helps!"

**Example - Not Good:**
> "This has been answered 100 times. Search before asking."

### Mistakes

Everyone makes mistakes:
- Code bugs ‚Üí Normal, just fix them
- Documentation errors ‚Üí Happens, submit a PR
- Said something wrong ‚Üí Apologize and move on
- Accidentally offensive ‚Üí Apologize sincerely, learn, improve

**Intent matters, but impact matters more.** If you hurt someone unintentionally:
1. Apologize
2. Learn why it was hurtful
3. Don't do it again
4. Move forward
